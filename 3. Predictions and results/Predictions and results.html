PREDICTIONS AND RESULTS

Scientific realism
From Wikipedia

“Scientific realism usually holds that science makes progress, i.e. scientific theories usually get successfully better, or, rather, answer more and more questions”

 In 2003 I was asked to produce a rough research agenda for the dipole neurology theory. This is the list of questions and predictions which I thought need investigated.
(Those that are blacked out part of another ongoing project separate to this one.)

In February 2004 I drew up Dipole neurology draft one. A 15 page unreferenced rough and exploratory piece, where i attempted to explain the brain dipole hypothesis as well as exploring a range of related ideas.

REFINED PREDICTIONS FROM DRAFT ONE.

I refined the prediction and question list predating draft one. To include ion channel flow as being implicated in the generation of a cortical dipole. I Was also certain there had to be a magnetic substance in the brain involved in its dipole structure. This idea has been proposed by Marcos Banaclocha in two papers, and i have expanded upon why a magnetic substance could assist brain function in my own paper.

A RESULT CONSISTENT WITH THE THEORY

In April 2004 I researched further and became aware that magnetite had been discovered throughout the brain and coating the cortex. This was certainly consistent with the theory and provided one means to solve the stuctural formation problem from draft one.  At that stage I was able to refine the hypothesus. If the Dipole theory is right, and the entire brain has such a structure. Then it does make two clear statements.

A Falsifiable one  :

There is no non electromagnetic mechanism or structure which explains the entire brains appearance, function and structures.

Universal predictions  :

If the entire brain has dipole or electromagnetic structure, then everything in it must also fall into such a structure.

A great deal of it’s functions and systems must be involved in the generation of that structure.

As I had now became aware of the massive amount of research available on brain lateralization. There appeared to be an opportunity to provide evidence for the universal predictions. I could expand the prediction on that basis.

EXPANDING ON THE UNIVERSAL PREDICTION

"That the majority of research which shows lateralization will uncover reversals processed  consistent with a dipoles poles no matter what kind of scale and system it is looking at. Ions, neurotransmitters, receptors, neuron structures, entire brain regions. More precisely …Towards the brains poles"

"In a sample of left brain hemispheres Most primary Neuron assemblies, neurochemicals, submodulation neurons and transmitters will have convergent (or inhibitory) properties"

"In a sample of right brain hemisphere Most Most primary Neuron assemblies, neurochemicals, submodulation neurons and transmitters will have divergent (or excitatory) properties"

Following from that Autumn 2004 I wrote a series of papers each going through a major brain system.  These lead to putting together Dipole Neurology Draft 2 in February 2005.
