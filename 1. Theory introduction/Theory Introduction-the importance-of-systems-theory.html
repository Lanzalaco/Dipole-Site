THE IMPORTANCE OF SYSTEMS THEORY IN THIS PROJECT

Central to this project is my analysis of the implication of complexity theory for studying the brain's structure. Using systems theory it has become clear there are some limitations in a strict use of the scientific method to understand an overtly complex system such as the brain.

The systems theory approach to understanding our reality and its complexity places great importance on finding both the linear and non-linear parts, in its area of focus. (Linear and non-linear are equivalent in everyday terms to words like order and chaos) Systems theorists are not afraid to encompass, these hard to define elements in their results. This approach has proved more successful than any other in understanding many complete pictures of how human populations, our planet or biology work. The majority of the scientific information availible on a complex organ like the brain concentrates on getting facts which get published if they fit the falsifiabity format of linear, ordered, and quantifiable. I wouldnt deny the importance of falsification process, except that it pre-dominates medical science so much that we have a fragmented view of the brain. Worst still we have settled for this incomplete picture over generations, simply because there are far too many restrictions on what we can propose .

“Physicists know that their methods, of analysis and logical reasoning can never explain the whole realm of natural phenomena at once, and so they single out a certain group of phenomena and try to build a model to describe this group. In doing so they neglect other phenomena and the model will therefore not give a complete description of the real situation. The phenomena which are not taken into account, may have either such a small effect that their inclusion would not alter the theory significantly, or they may be left out simply because they are not know the time at which the theory is built”

Fritjof Capra : Systems theorist

Its quite amazing that the answer to brain structure and function have been available on initial dissection all this time. The most obvious feature is the symmetry and the “field line” type patterns of inter Callosum axons. Why has it never been suggested that this is due to electromagnetism ? It probably was, many times but was rejected due to being "unfalsifiable". Since the 70's enough evidence has been availible to propose a brain dipole theory by looking at the hemisphere content. Due to the demands of falsification, something so simple, obvious and important, has been long overlooked.

“ The growing acceptance of the concept of self-organized criticality has seen its application to the study of diverse complex systems, varying from landslides, earth quakes, solar flares, ant nests and evolution in nature, to traffic jams, economies and wars in human civilization. The philosophy of this approach is notably different from that in the simple sciences. The most commonly accepted paradigm of scientific enquiry is arguably that of falsification, originally proposed by Karl Popper. In the conventional approach, a prediction is derived from the theory being tested. Scientists find out whether this prediction is supported by the real world. The theory is falsified – or at least needs to be modified – if the real world yields results contrary to the prediction. This approach applies quite well to the simple sciences. On the other hand, this is often not possible when the subject matter is complex    “

Soon Chun Siong
National University of Singapore
