SECTION GUIDE (Use index on left to open)

The Dipole Neurology project is a book in itself which is available on request. What is here is a series of section summaries from the book. The most important sections are 2 which illustrates the cortical dipole and 6 which proposes the limbic system's linear multipole.

NOTE: These sections are already outdated in many respects but have been left up to provide some online material. The current paper which represents the theory after being subject to peer reviews is downloadable from here.

NOTE : References are widely numbered due to being re-organized from several projects.

1. EASY READ SUMMARY

A two page summary of the theory.

2. DIPOLE CORRELATES WITH BRAIN STRUCTURE AND FUNCTION

I introduce the theory, with some background, and then present the initial case for looking at cortex brain structure more seriously as an electromagnetic system of opposites. I finish with a section to see if neuron components can build the system structures necessary for the theory by taking a detailed look at the microtubule numbering system, and what a dipole theory has to say about the entire cortex structure from atomic scale upwards.

3. NEUROCHEMICAL EVIDENCE FOR A BRAIN DIPOLE

Here we look at harder evidence for the idea that the entire brains content must be consistent with an electric dipole structure. Neurotransmitters,  receptors, ions, neuromodulators. Finishing with some conceptual diagrams to explain the dipole theory more fully.

4. MAGNETS ON THE BRAIN

Researchers in various fields have still not found where human short term memory is stored.  In this section I present evidence and concepts which illustrate that our brain is coated with a ferroelectric fluid capable of holding memories in layers. Ferrofluid gives missing pieces, and unifies many current theories of brain function. Neurodynamics, ORCH OR, Stochastic resonance, CEMI field, NAAMF’s (Neuronal activity associated magnetic fields)

5. DOES DIPOLE SOLVE BINDING COMPUTATION PROBLEM ?

The Ferrofluid outlined in the above section is ideal for short term quantum "style" superposition processing at body temperatures.  To do this I investigate neurophysiology of EEG, P300 and the computations of the thalamocortical system in order to understand if the whole brain processes occur in cycles of cortical superposition and thalamic spectral decomposition.

6. QUADRUPOLE STRUCTURES IN THE LIMBIC SYSTEM

The final bit of the brain puzzle, the limbic system is analysed and correlates well with a linear quadrupole. The inverse of a dipole.  Quadrupoles are looked at in greater detail, the limbic system is taken apart and modlled as a linear multipole. Finally this multipole model is put back in context with the outer brain dipole to get a greater idea how the whole brain operates as system.
